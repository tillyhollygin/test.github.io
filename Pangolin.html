  1. The pangolin is closely related to carnivores, but they are the only mammals that are covered in scales.
  2. Pangolin scales are made of keratin, just like our finger nails, and make up 20 per cent of their body weight.
  3. The word "pangolin" comes from the Malay word "penggulung" , which means "one that rolls up" .
  4. When it is threatened a pangolin will curl itself into a tight ball, which is impenetrable to predators.
  5. The mammal can consume up to 20,000 ants a day. That's about 73 million ants a year!
  6. Pangolin can close their ears and nostrils using strong muscles. This helps protect them from ant attacks.
